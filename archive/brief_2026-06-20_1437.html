详细中文报道

局势概览： 津巴布韦议会下院已通过一项具有争议的法案，该法案将把总统任期从五年延长至七年，并取消直接的总统选举，未来的总统将由议会选举产生。这项立法如果获得参议院批准并由总统签署成为法律，将可能使现任总统埃默森·姆南加古瓦（Emmerson Mnangagwa）的任期延长至2030年。 投票情况：在周四的投票中，超过200名议员投票赞成该法案，远超修宪所需的三分之二多数门槛。共有216名议员支持该立法，42人反对。 法案内容：除了延长总统任期外，该法案的核心内容还包括废除直接的总统选举制度。这意味着未来的国家元首将不再由全国选民直接投票选出，而是由议会议员投票决定。 背景与争议：姆南加古瓦总统现年83岁，自2017年以来一直掌权，并在2018年和2023年赢得了备受争议的选举。该法案是执政党津巴布韦非洲民族联盟-爱国阵线（Zanu-PF）推动修宪以延长总统任期的努力的顶峰，该计划在2月份获得了内阁支持。反对党、公民社会团体和宪法律师认为，如此重大的宪法变革应通过全民公投来决定，而不是仅仅通过议会批准。他们担心这些修正案可能削弱民主问责制。 执政党观点：支持者则认为，这些改革对于确保国家政治的连续性和稳定至关重要。
下一步观察： 该法案接下来将提交参议院审议，预计也将获得批准。最终，它需要总统姆南加古瓦的签署才能生效。外界将密切关注津巴布韦的政治走向，以及此次修宪对该国民主进程和未来政治格局的影响。

Detailed English brief

Zimbabwe's lower house of parliament has passed a bill that would extend presidential terms from five to seven years and abolish direct presidential elections, with future presidents to be chosen by parliament. If approved by the Senate and signed by the president, the bill would allow current President Emmerson Mnangagwa, 83, to potentially remain in power until 2030. The ruling Zanu-PF party-backed legislation passed with a significant majority, with 216 lawmakers voting in favor and 42 against. Opposition parties, civil society groups, and legal experts have voiced strong concerns, arguing that such fundamental constitutional changes should be decided by a national referendum rather than solely through parliamentary approval. They fear the amendments could weaken democratic accountability, while supporters maintain they are necessary for continuity and stability. Mnangagwa has been in power since 2017, following a military-backed ousting of Robert Mugabe, and won disputed elections in 2018 and 2023. The bill now moves to the Senate.

关注点 This legislative move in Zimbabwe represents a significant shift towards consolidating presidential power and potentially undermining democratic processes. The abolition of direct presidential elections and the extension of term limits raise serious concerns about the future of democracy and political accountability in the country, drawing criticism from opposition and civil society groups.
