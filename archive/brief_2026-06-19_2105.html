Detailed English brief

This legislation, if enacted, could enable current President Emmerson Mnangagwa, 83, to remain in power until 2030. The ruling Zanu-PF party has been pushing for this constitutional amendment, which received cabinet backing in February. Mnangagwa previously described himself as a constitutionalist and pledged to respect term limits. The bill passed with 216 votes in favor, exceeding the two-thirds majority required for constitutional amendments, with 42 votes against. Opposition parties, civil society groups, and lawyers argue that such fundamental changes should be decided by a national referendum, not solely by parliament. Mnangagwa's presidency, since he took power in 2017, has been marked by economic challenges, disputed elections, and concerns over democratic backsliding. Critics fear the amendments will weaken democratic accountability, while supporters claim they are necessary for continuity and stability. The 2013 constitution limited presidents to two terms and stipulated that any extension would require voter endorsement via referendum.

关注点 The passage of this bill in Zimbabwe represents a significant move towards consolidating power by the ruling party and potentially extending the tenure of the current president indefinitely. It raises serious concerns about democratic governance, electoral integrity, and the future political landscape of the country, moving away from established democratic norms like direct presidential elections and term limits. The opposition's call for a referendum highlights the contested nature of these changes and their potential impact on the country's democratic trajectory.
