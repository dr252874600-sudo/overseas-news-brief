Detailed English brief

His supporters view this as a validation of his economic reforms. However, the election was overshadowed by conflict and security concerns, with 143 polling stations failing to open due to safety issues, particularly in the populous Amhara and Oromia regions. Armed groups in these areas, such as the Fano militia and the Oromo Liberation Army, rejected the election and are engaged in ongoing fighting with government forces. Analysts note that while Ahmed initially promised to heal divisions, his actions against Tigrayan political elites and the subsequent civil war have exacerbated national fragmentation. Despite winning the Nobel Peace Prize for ending hostilities with Eritrea, the country faces escalating internal violence and security challenges. The low voter turnout and opposition boycotts raise questions about the representativeness of the election results. Security experts warn that Ethiopia could be heading towards renewed conflict, with ethnic tensions, regional separatism, and strained relations with neighbors posing significant challenges for Ahmed's second term.

关注点 The election outcome in Ethiopia, while consolidating Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed's power, occurs against a backdrop of significant internal conflict and regional instability. The ongoing violence in regions like Tigray, Amhara, and Oromia, coupled with strained relations with neighbors, poses a substantial risk to regional stability and could lead to a humanitarian crisis, impacting international aid efforts and geopolitical dynamics in the Horn of Africa.
