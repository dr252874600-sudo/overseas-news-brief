Lama Tsultrim, a 76-year-old Bon lama, still guards this ancient walnut tree, which is intertwined with the village's houses. However, climate-driven floods are eroding Lubra's land and destroying homes, also endangering the walnut tree that symbolizes the village's genesis. The local mud-brick houses are also at risk due to erosion. The Bon traditions in Lubra hold significant cultural importance, but the natural disasters brought by climate change pose a severe threat to their survival.

关注点 This event highlights the impact of climate change on cultural heritage and religious practices in remote regions. The specific case of Lubra village and the Bon faith demonstrates how environmental degradation can threaten the existence of unique traditions and communities, underscoring the need for climate adaptation strategies that consider cultural preservation.
