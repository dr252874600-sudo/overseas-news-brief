Detailed English brief

Public market reporting on June 15 showed a sharp decline in iron-ore prices, with some contracts falling more than 4 percent and benchmark indications approaching $98 a metric ton. The move reflected a combination of weaker steel-demand expectations, pressure on mill margins, inventory conditions and broader macroeconomic risk. When construction and manufacturing demand soften and steelmakers' profitability narrows, mills generally become less willing to secure expensive raw materials, placing downward pressure on ore prices. Middle East tensions and risks to major shipping routes can raise fuel, insurance and freight costs, but shipping risk does not automatically lift the commodity price when demand concerns are stronger. Available reports did not indicate an abrupt inventory change large enough to reverse the overall balance immediately. The next indicators to watch are Chinese apparent steel consumption, blast-furnace operating rates, port inventories, arrivals and mill margins. If finished-steel prices remain weak relative to raw-material costs, production cuts could become more likely and reduce purchasing demand for both iron ore and coking coal.

Fitch Ratings · 2026-06-15 11:25
详细中文报道

惠誉评级最新金属与矿业价格假设显示，优质炼焦煤价格预计为2026年每吨230美元、2027年190美元、2028年160美元和2029年140美元。公开资料同时显示，优质硬焦煤价格已从2022年约每吨423美元的高位回落至2026年5月约182美元。评级机构的长期价格假设主要用于评估矿企信用质量和现金流承受能力，并不等同于短期现货价格预测。其背后的逻辑包括新增供应逐渐释放、异常高价时期结束，以及全球钢铁需求增速趋于正常。对钢厂而言，焦煤价格下降有助于降低高炉炼铁成本，但实际收益还取决于焦炭价格、运输费用、钢材售价和产量水平。对焦煤生产商而言，价格中枢下移可能压缩利润和资本开支空间，高成本矿山承受的压力更明显。未来若供应中断、极端天气或主要出口国政策变化，价格仍可能偏离这一基准路径。

Detailed English brief

Fitch Ratings has revised its assumptions for premium coking coal to $230 a metric ton in 2026, $190 in 2027, $160 in 2028 and $140 in 2029. Publicly available figures also show that premium hard coking coal fell from an average near $423 a ton in 2022 to about $182 in May 2026. These assumptions are primarily used to test mining companies' credit profiles and long-term cash flows; they are not precise forecasts for the next spot-market transaction. The expected downward path reflects gradually improving supply, the normalization of prices after an exceptional period and more moderate growth in global steel demand. Lower coking-coal prices can reduce blast-furnace steelmaking costs, but the benefit to mills also depends on coke prices, freight, finished-steel prices and production rates. For coal producers, a lower long-term price environment can narrow margins and constrain capital spending, with higher-cost mines facing greater pressure. Supply disruptions, severe weather or policy changes in major exporting countries could still push actual prices away from Fitch's baseline.

关注点 这条信息适合用于钢企和焦煤企业的中长期盈利压力测试，而不应被当作短期买卖价格预测。
